Abstract

The recently emerging 3D/4D Gaussian Splatting (GS) has demonstrated transformative potential in enabling photorealistic virtualization. However, user behavior-aware studies remain limited due to the absence of large-scale, comprehensive datasets. To address this deficit, we introduce GSpark, the first large-scale multimodal user-behavior dataset covering multiple categories of 3DGS/4DGS content. We captured synchronized 6DoF motions and gaze signals from participants interacting with 27 diverse Gaussian Splatting scenes, facilitating research in viewport prediction, network streaming, and user identification.
